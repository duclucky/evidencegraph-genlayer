# EvidenceGraph — 2-minute demo script

## 0:00–0:15 — The problem

“Intelligent Contracts can reason about complex claims, but only if their evidence is useful. Builders currently bring scattered links, vague statements, and screenshots with no consistent quality check.”

Show the hero and briefly point to the positioning: EvidenceGraph prepares evidence; it does not decide the final outcome.

## 0:15–0:45 — Weak evidence

Scroll to the builder and click **Weak self-claim**. Explain that the submission has only a self-authored statement, no public URL, no independent source, and no timestamp. Click **Review evidence pack**.

Point out the low total, weak source quality, high manipulation risk, missing-proof list, and actionable recommendation.

## 0:45–1:25 — Strong milestone pack

Click **Strong milestone pack**. Walk through the public repository, timestamped release, documentation, demo, and test result. Explain how each source supports a different part of the claim and makes manipulation harder.

Click **Review evidence pack**. Highlight the five transparent sub-scores, evidence-ready verdict, strong source quality, low manipulation risk, and GenLayer-ready status.

## 1:25–1:50 — Structured output

Expand **Evidence package JSON**. Show that the claim, use case, evidence items, scores, gaps, and recommendation are normalized into one object. Point out the compact `contract_input` quality gate: this is the handoff an Intelligent Contract can consume for richer LLM and web-based review.

## 1:50–2:00 — Close

“EvidenceGraph is the quality layer before judgment: better evidence in, better reasoning out. The MVP runs entirely in the browser, with a deterministic Python reference engine and a clear path to GenLayer.”
